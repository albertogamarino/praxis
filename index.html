01 / The tension

The problem is not that teams use AI. It is that the workflow around AI remains implicit.

AI adoption creates operational ambiguity before it creates operating leverage. Praxis makes that ambiguity visible.

01

Execution moves faster.

AI accelerates drafting, research, analysis, reporting and synthesis. The workflow often still assumes human-only production.

02

Judgment becomes less visible.

Teams need to know where human judgment enters, what it verifies and when it overrides the system.

03

Control sits in the wrong place.

Senior review becomes the hidden safety mechanism. Escalation, evidence and audit trail are added after the fact.

02 / Praxis MAP

Praxis MAP makes the workflow visible.

A focused diagnostic for one to three AI-exposed workflows. MAP identifies how work currently moves, where AI is already entering, where friction accumulates, and where judgment, control, accountability and AI tooling logic need redesign.

Designed to be bought before a broader transformation.

MAP gives leadership a concrete view of the workflow before committing to redesign, tool rollout, agent development or operating model change.

Scope 1–3 workflows
Inputs Interviews, artefacts, policies, board materials, approvals
Outputs Flow Map, AI Exposure Map, Control Map, Roadmap
Decision Where AI belongs, what to redesign, which controls must exist
What MAP clarifies

Where AI can support the workflow, where it should not yet be used, what judgment must stay human, and what evidence or escalation is required.

What AI role belongs here

MAP does not start from vendor selection. It identifies the capability the workflow needs: drafting, triage, research, monitoring, evidence collection, control checking or escalation support.

Typical workflow examples

Contract review, board pack preparation, management reporting, regulatory monitoring, finance commentary, matter delivery, compliance review and approval flows.

What happens after MAP

Leadership can move into BLUEPRINT, test a redesign, create operating standards, configure existing tools, or consider a dedicated workflow agent only where the model requires it.

03 / Sequence

A sequence, not an open-ended advisory engagement.

MAP is the entry point. BLUEPRINT and EMBED follow only when the workflow evidence is clear enough to redesign, operationalize or support with targeted AI capabilities.

01 / MAP

Understand the work AI is entering.

Make the current workflow, AI exposure, friction, control gaps and candidate AI roles visible.

02 / BLUEPRINT

Redesign roles, controls and AI tooling logic.

Define who executes, who reviews, who decides, what evidence is required, where tooling belongs and whether the AI capability should be bought, configured or built.

03 / EMBED

Support adoption through operating rituals.

Help the redesigned workflow become governance routines, decision logs, review cadences, agent pilots and working behaviour.

Workflow agents

Agents are not the starting point. They are a possible implementation layer when the Blueprint shows that a dedicated AI capability is required inside a redesigned workflow.
