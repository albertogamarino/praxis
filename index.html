01 / The tension

The problem is not that teams use AI. It is that the workflow around AI remains implicit.

AI adoption creates operational ambiguity before it creates operating leverage. Praxis makes that ambiguity visible.

01

Execution moves faster.

AI accelerates drafting, research, analysis, reporting and synthesis. The workflow often still assumes human-only production.

02

Judgment becomes less visible.

Teams need to know where human judgment enters, what it verifies and when it overrides the system.

03

Control sits in the wrong place.

Senior review becomes the hidden safety mechanism. Escalation, evidence and audit trail are added after the fact.

02 / Praxis MAP

Praxis MAP makes the workflow visible.

A focused diagnostic for one to three AI-exposed workflows. MAP identifies how work currently moves, where AI is already entering, where friction accumulates, and where judgment, control and accountability need redesign.

Designed to be bought before a broader transformation.

MAP gives leadership a concrete view of the workflow before committing to redesign, tool rollout or operating model change.

Scope 1–3 workflows
Inputs Interviews, artefacts, policies, board materials, approvals
Outputs Flow Map, Opportunity Matrix, Control Map, Roadmap
Decision Where to use AI, what to redesign, which controls must exist
What MAP clarifies

Where AI can support the workflow, where it should not yet be used, what judgment must stay human, and what evidence or escalation is required.

Typical workflow examples

Contract review, board pack preparation, management reporting, regulatory monitoring, finance commentary, matter delivery, compliance review and approval flows.

What happens after MAP

Leadership can decide whether to move into BLUEPRINT, test a redesign, create operating standards, or stop AI adoption in parts of the workflow until controls exist.

03 / Sequence

A sequence, not an open-ended advisory engagement.

MAP is the entry point. BLUEPRINT and EMBED follow only when the workflow evidence is clear enough to redesign or operationalize.

01 / MAP

Understand the work AI is entering.

Make the current workflow, exposure, friction and control gaps visible.

02 / BLUEPRINT

Redesign roles, controls and escalation.

Define who executes, who reviews, who decides, what evidence is required and where tooling belongs.

03 / EMBED

Support adoption through operating rituals.

Help the redesigned workflow become governance routines, decision logs, review cadences and working behaviour.
