01 / Problem

You may need Praxis if AI is already changing how work gets done.

AI adoption often starts inside existing work: a draft, a report, a board pack, a contract review, a research memo. The process gets faster before the workflow gets clearer.

AI use is informal.

Teams are experimenting before review points and usage standards are clear.

Supervision is weaker.

Drafting, research or reporting moves faster while verification stays informal.

Senior people bottleneck.

Senior reviewers become the hidden control layer for uncertain work.

Accountability is unclear.

When AI contributes to output, ownership of quality becomes less explicit.

Controls are outdated.

Existing controls were designed for human-only workflows.

Pressure is rising.

Board, partner or executive demand for AI adoption arrives without a model.

Before adding more AI, map the work it is entering.

In high-trust workflows, AI changes who executes, who reviews, who decides, what evidence is required and where accountability sits.

03 / Method

MAP first. BLUEPRINT and EMBED only when the evidence is clear.

Praxis is sequenced to avoid abstract advisory work. Each stage answers a different operating question.

01 / Primary entry

MAP

Understand the work AI is entering.

02 / After MAP

BLUEPRINT

Redesign roles, controls, escalation paths, tooling logic and operating sequences.

03 / Selective

EMBED

Support adoption through governance routines, implementation support and operating rituals.

MAP clarifies how the workflow behaves today and what decisions leadership can make before redesigning or implementing AI.
BLUEPRINT turns MAP evidence into a target operating model: who executes, who reviews, who decides, what escalates and where tooling belongs.
EMBED supports the transition from design to operating reality through governance sessions, decision logs, review routines and implementation support.
