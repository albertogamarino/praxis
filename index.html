01 The Structural Shift Where the pressure is already visible
Diagnosis

Most organizations are optimizing a model being repriced beneath them.

AI compresses execution before organizations redesign roles, controls, evidence and accountability around it.

High performers are nearly 3× more likely to have fundamentally redesigned workflows. McKinsey identifies this as the single strongest predictor of EBIT impact from AI — stronger than tool selection, talent, or investment level. Most organizations haven't done it.

01

Execution accelerates. AI compresses drafting, analysis, research and synthesis. The workflow still assumes human-only production.

02

Judgment becomes exposed. Teams need to know where human judgment enters, what it verifies and when it overrides the system.

03

Controls lag behind. Evidence, escalation, audit trail and accountability are added after the fact instead of designed into the flow.

07 What This Is Not Positioning by exclusion
Exclusions

PRAXIS is workflow redesign for high-trust work. Not generic AI adoption.

Not AI tool implementation
The right AI role cannot be determined before the workflow is mapped. Tool selection follows from the Blueprint — not the other way around.
Not agent development by default
Agents are an output of the Blueprint, not an entry point. PRAXIS does not build agents into workflows that have not been redesigned around them. An agent deployed into an unredesigned workflow inherits every control gap already in it.
Not adoption without redesign
Deploying AI into an existing workflow makes a poorly designed process faster. Map first. The sequence is not optional.
Not a large-firm engagement
Large firm AI recommendations are shaped by vendor relationships and engagement economics before the analysis begins. PRAXIS has none of these constraints. The finding can be: no new platform required.
